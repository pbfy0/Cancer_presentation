Brain cancer

What is cancer?

Cancer is a mutation in a cell that makes it divide uncontrollably. Your cells divide all the time, but cancerous cells do so continuosly. It then can mutate farther to split even faster.

This happens repeatedly, and eventually blood vessels can grow around the tumor, supplying it with more nutrients to grow even faster. Also, mutated calls can travel through the bloodstream and infect other parts of the body.

Risk factors

Brain cancer has no well known risk factors, but several things can affect your likelyhood of getting it.
  • White people get brain cancer more commonly
  • Older people get brain cancer more frequently
  • Ionizing radiation can cause brain cancer
  • Some chemicals from jobs can cause cancer
  • Family history can have some effect

Treatments

  • Surgery
  • Radiation therapy: Kill the tumor with high energy radiation
  • Chemotherapy: Kill the tumor with drugs
      • Multiple treatments are usually used together to increase the change of survival.

    Brain cancer can form a tumor anywhere in your brain. There are no other natural known causes of brain cancer other than vinyl chloride or ionizing radiation. Cell phone radiation is a possible cause, but this is uncertain. People with some inherited diseases like neurofibromatosis are more likely to develop tumors.

    Symptoms

    • Headache
    • Rapid decline in school results
    • Early morning vomiting
    • Behavioral changes
    • High blood pressure
    Brain cancer is approximately 50% more common in men. It is more common in the middle states, and less in the northeast and southwest. About 13,700 people die per year of brain cancer, 59% of the 22,910 people diagnosed
